# Agrocart: Go-To-Market (GTM) Strategy & 90-Day Rollout

*This document solves the "Cold Start Problem" of launching a two-sided marketplace. Investors want to know exactly how Agrocart will acquire its first critical mass of users without spending millions on generic marketing.*

---

## 1. The Core Strategy: "Demand-Led, Hyper-Local"
Marketplaces fail when they try to launch everywhere at once. If a transporter opens the app in Enugu and there are no loads, they will delete the app and never return. 

**The Rule:** Agrocart will **not** launch nationwide. We will launch on **one specific route** with **one specific crop** for the first 90 days. 

*   **Target Crop:** Maize (High volume, staple crop, massive corporate demand).
*   **Target Route:** Kaduna (High Production) to Lagos/Ogun (High Industrial Processing).

---

## 2. The 90-Day Tactical Rollout

### Phase 1: Securing the Anchor (Days 1 - 30)
We must secure the "Demand" before we recruit the "Supply." Transporters will only sign up if guaranteed loads are waiting.

*   **The Target:** Large industrial processors (e.g., Flour Mills of Nigeria, Olam, or regional feed mills).
*   **The Tactic (Enterprise Sales):** Use the `AGROCART_CORPORATE_LOI.md`. Pitch the Procurement Directors not on "finding trucks," but on the **Quality Vault** and **Traceability**. 
*   **The Goal:** Secure 1 or 2 signed LOIs guaranteeing that if Agrocart can provide trucks for 1,000 tons of Maize from Kaduna, they will pay through our platform.

### Phase 2: The Physical Hub & The Farmers (Days 30 - 60)
With the LOI in hand, we set up the physical intake point to capture the supply.

*   **The Tactic (Boots on the Ground):** We lease a small, existing warehouse or aggregation center in a high-density farming belt in Kaduna. This becomes "Agrocart Hub Alpha."
*   **Acquiring Farmers:** We hire 3 local "Agronomist Agents" who speak Hausa fluently. They visit local farming cooperatives and offer a simple deal: *"Bring your maize to Hub Alpha. We grade it fairly, give you a digital receipt, and guarantee a buyer within 48 hours."*
*   **The Goal:** Aggregate 500 tons of Maize physically sitting at the Hub, graded and entered into the software.

### Phase 3: Acquiring the Fleet (Days 60 - 90)
We now have guaranteed corporate demand in Lagos and physical supply sitting in Kaduna. Now we recruit the trucks.

*   **The Tactic (Truck Park Infiltration):** Digital marketing (Facebook/Instagram) does not work for independent African truck drivers. We hire "Fleet Activation Agents." 
*   **The Execution:** Agents physically go to major truck parks in Kaduna. They approach drivers who are waiting empty. They install the Agrocart App on the driver's phone, help them upload their driver's license (for KYC), and immediately show them the Load Board containing the 500 tons of Maize waiting at Hub Alpha.
*   **The Subsidy (The Hook):** For their first 3 trips, Agrocart waives its 5% commission and offers a "Fast Payout Guarantee" (subsidized by our VC funding) so the driver gets paid instantly upon delivery, proving the system is better than traditional cash brokers.
*   **The Goal:** Onboard 50 Verified Transporters and complete the delivery of the 500 tons to the Corporate Anchor in Lagos.

---

## 3. Key Milestones for the First 90 Days
If we achieve these metrics, raising a Seed Round of $2M+ becomes highly probable.

1.  **Demand:** 1 Corporate LOI signed.
2.  **Supply:** 50 Farmers digitized with their first "Agro-Score."
3.  **Fleet:** 50 Verified Transporters.
4.  **Transaction Volume:** ~30 successful trips completed with zero cash fraud and 100% escrow release.

---
## 4. The Flywheel Effect (Post-90 Days)
Once the first 30 trips are completed successfully:
*   Transporters tell other transporters about the "Instant Escrow Payouts." Acquisition costs drop.
*   Farmers tell other farmers about the fair grading and fast off-take.
*   The Corporate Buyer sees the QR Traceability and mandates that *all* their suppliers must use Agrocart. The system begins to scale organically.
